Scientific Interpretation Guide (3D MS1 Peak Envelope)

The interactive 3D map shows individual MS1 signal points acquired around the chromatographic peak apex (±0.05 Da mass window and ±30 seconds retention time window). Signal intensity is encoded by both point size and the high-contrast neon colorscale (Cyan → Pink → Gold).

  • Clean Chemical Peak (Perfect Co-elution): Displays a smooth, three-dimensional envelope centered on a single apex point of maximum intensity, with values dropping off symmetrically in both RT and m/z.
  • Plausible Adducts / Co-eluting Ions: Genuine adduct ions will share the exact same chromatographic elution profile in RT but will be shifted on the m/z axis (e.g., [M+Na]+ shifted by +22.9892 Da relative to [M+H]+).
  • Background Contamination / Streaks: Continuous signals that do not show a clear peak apex in RT but rather a flat line of signal across the entire time range are typical chemical background contaminants (e.g., plasticizers, detergents).
  • Matrix Noise / Spurious Signals: Isolated, low-intensity points scattered randomly without a defined Gaussian-like peak shape represent electronic noise or matrix background.
